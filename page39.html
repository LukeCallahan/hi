HELPING
A MENTALLY ILL LOVED ONE
IN A COUNTY JAIL

by Barry T. Schnell, M.A.


This book is written to educate and empower families and
friends of the 800,000 mentally ill individuals who are incarcerated in county jails and juvenile detention centers
across the country each year. When a mentally ill person is confined in a county jail it can be a life threatening, traumatizing and predatory experience.

Can you help them? The answer is ABSOLUTELY!
This publication will help you navigate through the turmoil and provide substantive assistance to a mentally ill loved one who is withering behind bars in a county jail or juvenile detention center.

You must do all you can to protect the safety, well-being and legal rights of your incarcerated, mentally ill family member. This book is written in a down to earth style and is easy to read and understand. It has an encouraging, informal tone and similarly accessible books are virtually nonexistent.

Public libraries may want to order reference as well as circulating copies for their branches.




ABOUT THE AUTHOR

This book is written by Barry T. Schnell. He graduated from The Pennsylvania State University and received an M.A. Degree from Rider University. He studied for two years on a Doctoral Fellowship at Penn State. He also studied a year in the Doctoral Program at Lehigh University.

For 15 years the author worked as an Administrative Analyst for the Family Courts of New Jersey. He has had several books published that help teenage parents acquire social services. He has also written a book explaining to parents how to enforce their child support rights through the courts.

The author has done volunteer work as a Literacy Volunteer and is an advocate for the decriminalization of mental illness.
