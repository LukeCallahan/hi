Lesson 1

EGO, THE SUBSTANCE OF HANDWRITING ANALYSIS

Gracious Permission to use this material has been given to me by the authors, Eldene Whiting CG and Paula Sassi CG

Introducing Capital Letters, Capital I, and Signature

The self image of every human being is the most driving motivation for each act they performs. From outrageous conceit through mature self realization to shy reluctance, the human ego is both a compelling force and a fragile essential.


A human being, however, is made up of many other facets as well. A person's intellect dictates how they solve their problems, how they copes with life and, to some extent, with whom they does all this. Their physical health and creative drive motivates most of their actions. All these affect their ego; they are interdependent.


Each experience one encounters and deals with in life teaches the lessons of life. We often class these lessons as the"negative" things that happen to us. To avoid the pain and fear of negative happenings we take a different approach or method the next time similar circumstances arise. We learn by experience.


Depending on the power of the intellect, the physical health and the strength of the ego, we overcome, we cope, or we succumb to anxieties, fears, inhibitions and repressions. We learn to control one part of our nature in order to prevent the discomfort of the negative experience. When we succeed the ego is bolstered and the personality is strengthened.


Within the handwriting we look for strengths and weaknesses of ego in capital letters, the capital I in particular, and also in the size and shape of the signature.


Capital letters reflect the writer's ego interaction with others and how a person fits themself into the societal environment.The capital I is an excellent gauge of the writer's reputation with himself. A person's signature is the personality they wish to present to the world. Through these graphic pictures in their writing, each writer tells the experienced analyst the story of their personal ego.

Vocabulary Help

SIGNATURE CONGRUENCY: The similarity of the signature with the body of the writing.


PPI: The personal pronoun which signifies the capital I
referring to first person singular.

INTERDEPENDENT: The reliance of one factor upon another which, in turn, creates a stronger or weaker personality trait.


EMPIRICAL: Originating in, or based on, observation or experience.
